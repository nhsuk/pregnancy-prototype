Weeks

1 to 8

In the first 4 weeks of pregnancy, you probably won't notice any symptoms. See your Gp as soon as you find out.

Weeks

9 to 12

IMPORTANT:
You'll be offered tests and scans including one to find the baby's chance of having Down's syndrome.

Weeks

13 to 16

You may have backache in pregnancy as your womb gets heavier and pregnancy hormones affect the ligaments in your body.

Weeks

17 to 20

This detailed ultrasound scan, sometimes called the mid-pregnancy scan, is usually carried out when you're between 18 and 21 weeks pregnant.

Weeks

21 to 24

By the time you're 24 weeks pregnant, the baby has a chance of survival if they are born.

Weeks

25 to 28

Download a birth plan template to fill in and save, stating your preferences for labour and birth.

Weeks

29 to 32

You may find it hard to sleep because you can't get comfortable. You might also find you need to pee a lot.

Weeks

33 to 36

Pre-eclampsia is a condition that affects some women in pregnancy, usually after 20 weeks, and it can be serious.

Weeks

37 to 42

Most women go into labour between 38 and 42 weeks of pregnancy.
