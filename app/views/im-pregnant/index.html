I'm pregnant Pregnancy

Everything you need to know about being pregnant is here. You'll find detailed week-by-week guides and lots of information about each stage of your pregnancy.

Your pregnancy week by week

Weeks

1 to 8

In the first 4 weeks of pregnancy, you probably won't notice any symptoms. Your weeks of pregnancy are dated from the first day of your last period.

Weeks

9 to 12

Important: You'll be offered various tests and scans including screening to find the baby's chance of having Down's syndrome.

Weeks

13 to 16

You may have backache in pregnancy as your womb gets heavier and pregnancy hormones affect the ligaments in your body.

Weeks

17 to 20

Abdominal or stomach pain in pregnancy are common and some women also get headaches in pregnancy which are common.

Weeks

21 to 24

Not everybody gets stretch marks, but if you do, they will probably start becoming noticeable when you're around 22 to 24 weeks pregnant.

Weeks

25 to 28

Download a birth plan template to fill in and save, stating your preferences for labour and birth.

Weeks

29 to 32

You may find it hard to sleep because you can't get comfortable. You might also find you need to pee a lot.

Weeks

33 to 36

Pre-eclampsia is a condition that affects some women in pregnancy, usually after 20 weeks, and it can be serious.

Weeks

37 to 40

Most women go into labour between 38 and 42 weeks of pregnancy.

Weeks

40 plus

Most women go into labour naturally by the time they're 42 weeks pregnant.
